# AMC 12 & COMC Techniques Document Evaluation (Updated 2025)

Your 40-technique document provides a **solid foundational coverage** for AMC 12 and COMC preparation, but it contains critical gaps, outdated weightings, and category issues that limit its effectiveness for recent contest trends. The list currently addresses many core topics but only covers an estimated **60-70%** of recent AMC 12/COMC problems, not the 75% previously claimed. Strategic updates are needed to reflect **evolving contest patterns** and ensure comprehensive coverage.

## Key Evolution in AMC 12 (2020--2024) and COMC

Recent years have seen notable shifts in both the AMC 12 and the Canadian Open Mathematics Challenge (COMC):

-   **Algebra is still significant but not as dominant:** Earlier estimates of 42% algebra problems were too high. In practice, algebra comprises roughly one-third of AMC 12 questions[\[1\]](https://blog.thethinkacademy.com/mastering-the-amc-10-insights-from-2022-and-strategies-for-2023/#:~:text=2023%20blog,and%20number%20theory%20%2812), and similar proportions on COMC, rather than nearly half. This 6--10% overestimation suggests prior data may have been outdated or conflated with other contests[\[2\]](https://data.mendeley.com/datasets/b57t9psp65#:~:text=This%20is%20a%20primary%20dataset,Probability). Both contests still heavily feature algebra, but not to the exclusion of other topics.

-   **Number Theory has risen sharply:** The surge in number theory problems is real. AMC 12 went from minimal number theory to about **20% of problems by 2024**, with some exams reaching \~25%[\[3\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=,%E2%80%93%20PIE%2C%20bijections%2C%20constructive%20recursion). This upward trajectory (mirrored in COMC) confirms number theory as the second-most emphasized topic in recent years. The original document's recognition of this trend was accurate, though it's the upper end of the range[\[3\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=,%E2%80%93%20PIE%2C%20bijections%2C%20constructive%20recursion).

-   **Difficulty distribution has shifted:** The traditional easy-to-hard progression is less predictable now. Early problems (Questions 1--5) are no longer uniformly trivial -- some require nontrivial insights or careful reading[\[4\]](https://blog.thethinkacademy.com/2024-amc-10-exam-analysis-module-breakdown-difficulty-trends-and-prep-plan/#:~:text=,prepared%2C%20fast%2C%20and%20accurate). Conversely, later problems (20--25) have become slightly more accessible to well-prepared students. A 2024 analysis noted "the beginning is no longer easy, and the end is more accessible," rewarding students who are fast and accurate throughout[\[4\]](https://blog.thethinkacademy.com/2024-amc-10-exam-analysis-module-breakdown-difficulty-trends-and-prep-plan/#:~:text=,prepared%2C%20fast%2C%20and%20accurate). COMC, with its Parts A to C format, similarly demands focus even on "basic" Part A questions and offers relatively approachable advanced questions to those with strong fundamentals.

-   **Cross-topic integration is now common:** Modern AMC 12 and COMC problems often blend multiple areas of math in one question. For example, a geometry problem might require a number theoretic modular reasoning, or an algebra problem might involve combinatorial reasoning. Preparation guides emphasize *combining multiple topics* and adapting formulas in new ways[\[5\]](https://blog.thethinkacademy.com/how-to-prepare-for-amc-10-math-competition/#:~:text=Academy%20blog,Regular%20practice%20with). In essence, the contests are testing flexible problem-solving skills rather than isolated technique application.

These evolutions mean that an effective techniques list must be broad, up-to-date, and emphasize connections between topics.

## Major Gaps in the Current Technique List

Despite covering many fundamentals, the current 40-technique list misses several **high-probability techniques** and **advanced methods** that have grown in importance for AMC 12/COMC:

### Critical Missing High-Probability Techniques

-   **Vieta's Formulas (High priority):** Vieta's should be listed as a top-tier algebra technique. It appears frequently (almost every other contest) in polynomial root problems and symmetric sum questions. Many recent AMC 12 problems exploit Vieta's relationships, making it indispensable[\[6\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=Here%27s%20what%20the%20AMC%2012,covers). The original list underweighted this---treating Vieta's as a medium priority despite its ubiquity was a mistake.

-   **Linearity of Expectation:** This powerful probability method is absent from your list. Advanced AMC 12 and COMC probability questions often become tractable by summing expectations instead of brute-force casework. Top resources explicitly include **Linearity of Expectation** as a key concept for AMC 10/12 preparation[\[7\]](https://www.omegalearn.org/amc10#:~:text=5,Markov%20Chains%2C%20Cyclic%20Quadrilaterals%2C%20etc). Its omission leaves a gap in tackling expected value problems efficiently.

-   **Advanced Geometry Theorems:** Beyond basic triangle properties and Power of a Point, the list lacks higher-level geometry tools. **Power of a Point** itself deserves high priority (it's rightly in your list, but should be emphasized as core). In addition, techniques like the **Radical Axis theorem**, **Spiral Similarity**, and even elementary **Inversion** principles have surfaced in tough geometry problems. While these appear in \<10% of AMC 12 problems (more common in AIME/Olympiad), familiarity can give students an edge on the hardest geometry questions[\[6\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=Here%27s%20what%20the%20AMC%2012,covers). COMC Part C problems especially may reward creative use of these advanced theorems.

-   **Permutation & Combination Strategies:** The list covers basic counting, but missing is the **Linearity of Expectation** (mentioned above) and more nuanced counting methods like constructive counting and complementary counting beyond simple formulas. Also, the **Bijection principle** should be added -- it's a powerful way to simplify counting by one-to-one mapping, highlighted in many combinatorics problems and emphasized in coaching materials[\[8\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=,%E2%80%93%20PIE%2C%20bijections%2C%20constructive%20recursion).

### Important Medium-Probability Additions

-   **Lifting The Exponent Lemma (LTE):** An advanced number theory tool useful for exponent-heavy Diophantine equations. It rarely appears explicitly on AMC 12 (estimated \< 10% frequency), but knowing its "shortcuts" for powers and factors can simplify problems that would otherwise be very difficult[\[3\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=,%E2%80%93%20PIE%2C%20bijections%2C%20constructive%20recursion). For instance, problems involving orders of primes or large exponents might be cracked faster with LTE insights. This is more of an AIME-level technique, but top AMC 12 scorers (and COMC participants) benefit from exposure to LTE-style reasoning.

-   **Barycentric Coordinates / Mass Points:** These are modern geometry methods that can solve complex ratio problems elegantly. They have been gaining popularity in solutions for contest geometry. While not common in official AMC 12 problem statements, barycentric coordinates (and the simpler Mass Points method) are now taught in some high-level AMC 12 courses[\[3\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=,%E2%80%93%20PIE%2C%20bijections%2C%20constructive%20recursion). Including one of these methods in your list would fill the gap in leveraging coordinates and ratios in geometry problems.

-   **Recursion and Generating Functions:** The list should include techniques for complex sequences and counting problems. **Recursive thinking** (breaking problems into smaller sub-problems) is often needed for multi-step combinatorics problems. Similarly, **Generating functions**, though an advanced topic, occasionally provide elegant solutions or insights for counting problems on contests like COMC. These are medium-priority since they don't appear frequently in AMC 12, but when they do, they can be the key to solving an otherwise intractable problem.

-   **Inclusion-Exclusion Principle (PIE):** This is actually already in your list, but it deserves elevation. PIE appears often in complex counting problems (overlapping sets, distributions with restrictions) and is fundamental enough that it should be emphasized as a core technique rather than just one of many. Its correct application can solve a wide range of problems efficiently[\[8\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=,%E2%80%93%20PIE%2C%20bijections%2C%20constructive%20recursion).

By incorporating the above techniques, the list will better mirror the full spectrum of problem-solving tools that high scorers on AMC 12 and COMC typically command.

## Categorization and Priority Adjustments

Several techniques in the current list are miscategorized in terms of priority. Based on frequency in contests and importance:

-   **Upgrade to High Priority:**

-   *Vieta's Formulas* -- as noted, appears very frequently and underpins many algebra problems[\[6\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=Here%27s%20what%20the%20AMC%2012,covers).

-   *Power of a Point (PoP)* -- central to circle geometry (tangent/secant and chord power problems show up regularly).

-   *Inclusion-Exclusion (PIE)* -- fundamental for counting with constraints.

-   *Cauchy-Schwarz Inequality* -- while not extremely common, it has appeared in optimization problems and is a staple in advanced problem solving; worth listing as high priority given its broad utility and increasing use in contests' inequality or bounding problems.

-   **Downgrade to Medium Priority:**

-   *Hockey Stick Identity* -- a nice combinatorial identity, but specific and less frequently a linchpin in problems than, say, binomial coefficient properties in general (the identity might solve a small part of a problem; it's not as universally needed).

-   *Vandermonde's Identity* -- also specific; useful in combinatorial sums, but not often directly required.

-   *Derangements (subfactorial formula)* -- while derangements are a known classic problem, they appear infrequently. General counting strategies cover these, and a dedicated formula is not often needed on AMC 12/COMC questions.

These recategorizations align the technique emphasis with actual contest occurrence rates. For example, prioritizing Vieta's and PoP corresponds to their frequent appearance, whereas niche identities like Vandermonde's can be demoted to make room for more broadly useful tools.

**Numbering issues:** Note that the original list had some numbering errors (a duplicate #18 and an omission). These should be corrected for clarity, especially if the list is shared with students. A clean, correctly numbered list helps reference each technique unambiguously.

## Topic Coverage Assessment

Breaking down the list by subject area reveals strengths and areas to bolster:

### Algebra (Strong Core Coverage, Minor Gaps)

Algebra accounts for roughly 33% of recent AMC 12 problems (not 42% as previously claimed)[\[1\]](https://blog.thethinkacademy.com/mastering-the-amc-10-insights-from-2022-and-strategies-for-2023/#:~:text=2023%20blog,and%20number%20theory%20%2812), and your document covers many of the standard algebraic techniques well. Topics like quadratic manipulation (completing the square), systems of equations, inequalities, and modular arithmetic are appropriately included and are indeed frequently tested.

**Gaps:** Two areas need reinforcement: - **Functional Equations:** While not extremely common, functional equations have appeared in both AMC 12 and COMC (especially in COMC Part B/C) in forms like recursive definitions or satisfying certain properties. Extending the list to include strategies for functional equations (e.g. plugging in strategic values, swapping variables) would be beneficial. - **Sequences and Series:** The document could better cover advanced sequence analysis. Basic arithmetic/geometric sequences are easy, but contests have featured recursive sequences or series summations that require clever telescoping or using generating functions. Including techniques for handling non-trivial recurrences or series (like the method of differences, or recognizing famous sequences) would address this.

Overall, algebra is well-covered at the basic and intermediate level; just ensure the high-end topics like polynomial roots (Vieta's) and functional equations are given their due.

### Geometry (Adequate Basics, Missing Advanced Methods)

Geometry typically constitutes 20--25% of AMC 12 problems (and COMC usually has a couple of geometry questions in each section). Your list includes fundamental geometry (triangle properties, circle theorems, coordinate geometry basics), which covers the needs of most problems up to Q15.

**Gaps in advanced geometry:** - **Key Theorems:** Important results like **Ceva's Theorem**, **Menelaus' Theorem**, and **Stewart's Theorem** are absent. These theorems can be invaluable for non-intuitive geometry configurations (e.g., Ceva/Menelaus in concurrency and collinearity problems around Q20--Q25). They don't appear by name in problems, but having them in one's toolkit can simplify or unlock a solution. - **Transformational and Coordinate Techniques:** The list could mention using transformations (reflective symmetry, rotations) or coordinate bashes for geometry. Some problems that seem difficult synthetically become straightforward by placing points on a coordinate plane or applying complex numbers to transform geometry into algebra[\[6\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=Here%27s%20what%20the%20AMC%2012,covers). For instance, understanding how to use complex numbers for geometry or vectors for displacements can solve tricky geometry problems that pure Euclidean methods struggle with. - **Modern methods:** As discussed, **Barycentric coordinates** or **Mass Points** can solve ratio problems elegantly. Similarly, familiarity with the concept of **Spiral Similarity** (frequently used in solutions for configuration problems) can be a game-changer[\[6\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=Here%27s%20what%20the%20AMC%2012,covers). These are advanced tools; while not every student will master them, including them in the document (perhaps as optional enrichment) would keep the document in line with what top performers study.

By adding these, the document would better serve students aiming for the later geometry problems on AMC 12 and especially those tackling the more challenging geometry on COMC Part B/C.

### Number Theory (Good Foundation, Lacking Recent Emphasis)

Your list covers classic number theory: divisibility, modular arithmetic, prime factorization, and basic Diophantine equations. These indeed form the backbone of contest number theory and have become increasingly important (now around 20% of questions).

**Gaps in advanced number theory:** - **Orders and Fermat's Little Theorem**: The list should explicitly include using **modular arithmetic at an advanced level**, such as finding the order of an element (useful for repeating decimal or power cycle problems) and applying theorems like Fermat's Little Theorem and Euler's Totient Theorem. Some recent AMC 12 problems on remainders and exponents lean on these concepts[\[3\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=,%E2%80%93%20PIE%2C%20bijections%2C%20constructive%20recursion). - **Lifting Exponent / LTE**: As noted, while full LTE is above AMC 12 level, a simplified inclusion of *lifting exponents* tricks (like evaluating \$v_p(a\^n - b\^n)\$) could be useful for select problems. Even if not explicitly needed, exposure to this thinking helps in understanding why certain divisibility patterns hold. - **Quadratic Residues and Mods**: Concepts like quadratic residues or basic applications of the Chinese Remainder Theorem occasionally appear. These are medium-priority, but including a mention would round out the NT section.

The document's foundation is solid here; focusing on the rising topics (exponentiation and modular arithmetic tricks) will align it with contest trends.

### Combinatorics & Probability (Strong Basics, Missing Advanced Strategies)

The list covers counting principles (permutations, combinations, basic probability, etc.) which addresses straightforward problems usually found in the first 10--15 questions. This matches the \~15--20% of questions that are pure counting/probability.

**Gaps:** - **Inclusion-Exclusion Principle** -- should be emphasized (as mentioned in priority section) since it's fundamental to many complex counting tasks[\[8\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=,%E2%80%93%20PIE%2C%20bijections%2C%20constructive%20recursion). - **Constructive Counting and Bijections** -- Many harder counting problems are solved by clever constructions (e.g., distributing objects with constraints by constructing a bijection to an easier problem). This thinking should be encouraged by including it explicitly as a technique. For example, understanding how to construct a one-to-one correspondence (bijection) between complicated configurations and simpler ones can crack problems that at first seem unsolvable. - **Generating Functions** -- While typically beyond AMC 12, generating functions have occasionally been used in official solutions or by test-takers for tricky sequences and partitions. For COMC, which can have very challenging combinatorics in Part C, generating functions might be a viable approach. Even a basic introduction (using them for smaller counting problems or to derive formulas like Fibonacci or partition numbers) would elevate the document's completeness in combinatorics.

-   **Expected Value Techniques** -- Besides linearity of expectation, mention using complementary probability and symmetry in probability problems. These are common techniques when direct computation is hard (for instance, using symmetry arguments to solve states in a Markov chain-like process, which AMC 12 has tested). These strategies separate top solvers in probability questions.

### Trigonometry and Complex Numbers (Needs Slight Enhancement)

The current list likely includes standard trigonometry (law of sines, cosines, basic identities), which is necessary since AMC 12 explicitly allows trigonometry and typically has 1--2 trig problems. COMC also can include trigonometric arguments in geometry.

**Suggestions to enhance:** - **Advanced Trig Identities**: Include sum-to-product and product-to-sum identities, double-angle and half-angle identities. Occasionally, problems (especially around Q20) involve simplifying a product of sines/cosines or evaluating a telescoping trigonometric sum. - **Complex Numbers in Algebra/Geometry**: Consider adding the use of complex numbers as a method. For algebra, this includes understanding the nth roots of unity, which can solve certain polynomial equations elegantly. For geometry, using complex numbers to represent points can transform a geometry problem into algebra. While complex number techniques are not common on AMC 12, they provide a unifying approach for certain problems (e.g., solving a geometry problem by rotation in the complex plane). At the very least, recognizing when a problem is essentially asking for roots of unity or a complex number property is a plus for contestants.

In summary, the document should maintain its strong coverage of basics in all topics but update to include these advanced or recently-relevant techniques. This ensures students are not caught off guard by newer styles of problems.

## Recommendations for 2025 Updates

In light of the above findings, here are concrete recommendations to enhance and correct the techniques document for AMC 12 and COMC preparation:

### 1. Add and Emphasize High-Priority Techniques

Incorporate the missing high-impact techniques and mark them as such:

-   **Vieta's Formulas and Symmetric Polynomials:** Emphasize Vieta's role in connecting coefficients and roots, and extend to symmetric sums of roots. Many polynomial problems boil down to recognizing these relations[\[6\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=Here%27s%20what%20the%20AMC%2012,covers).

-   **Linearity of Expectation:** Provide a dedicated section on this principle for probability. Include a simple example to show how computing an expected value directly (via summing contributions of each random event) is often easier than enumerating outcomes[\[7\]](https://www.omegalearn.org/amc10#:~:text=5,Markov%20Chains%2C%20Cyclic%20Quadrilaterals%2C%20etc).

-   **Advanced Geometry Tools:** Add *Radical Axis Theorem* as an extension of PoP (since it's essentially the power of a point relative to two circles). Mention *Spiral Similarity* for solving problems involving similar triangles in circles, and note *Inversion* as an ambitious addition for tackling circle problems (perhaps as an appendix or enrichment topic, with a clear note that it's rarely needed at AMC 12 level).

-   **Barycentric Coordinates / Mass Points:** Introduce one of these methods for solving ratio problems. Mass points could be introduced as a more accessible technique to handle weighted points and cevians in triangles, while barycentric can be noted for those exploring deeper geometry[\[6\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=Here%27s%20what%20the%20AMC%2012,covers).

-   **Recursion and Bijection in Combinatorics:** Add examples of constructing recurrences for counting problems and solving them (or at least setting them up). Similarly, illustrate a bijection solution to a counting problem to show its power. These additions align with what high-end training materials cover[\[8\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=,%E2%80%93%20PIE%2C%20bijections%2C%20constructive%20recursion) and prepare students for non-standard combinatorics questions.

By making these additions, your document will cover the techniques heavily featured in contests through 2024 and anticipated in 2025.

### 2. Correct Technique Prioritization and Balance

Revisit the categorization of techniques into high vs. medium priority. The high-priority category should roughly mirror the distribution of contest problems: - Aim for about 50--60% of the high-priority techniques to be Algebra and Geometry (since together they constitute roughly 60% of problems on average). This includes the most commonly used strategies in those areas (e.g., Vieta's, PoP, coordinate geometry). - The remaining 40--50% of high-priority techniques should cover Number Theory and Combinatorics/Probability. For example, modular arithmetic, prime factorization tricks, PIE, and symmetry/expectation methods in probability should be high-priority given their frequent applicability.

Conversely, some of the highly specialized formulas or identities can be categorized as medium or low priority, as discussed. This rebalancing ensures students spend time on the techniques most likely to appear.

### 3. Emphasize Integrated Problem-Solving

Add a preface or notes within the document about **combining techniques**. Many recent problems require using two or three different ideas in tandem. For instance, an AMC 12 geometry problem might require setting up an equation (algebra) from a geometry configuration and then solving it using number theory modular reasoning. Training materials highlight the need to "translate word problems into equations, *combine multiple topics*, and adapt familiar formulas in new ways"[\[5\]](https://blog.thethinkacademy.com/how-to-prepare-for-amc-10-math-competition/#:~:text=Academy%20blog,Regular%20practice%20with).

Practically, you might include a section of **Mixed Techniques Practice** -- a few example problems or scenarios where multiple techniques from the list are used together. This will encourage readers to think beyond silos. Even simply noting in the description of a technique how it often pairs with another (e.g., using coordinate geometry along with algebraic distance formulas, or using modular arithmetic in a combinatorics problem about counting residues) will underscore this integrated approach.

For COMC specifically, integration is crucial: Part C problems especially often require a synthesis of ideas (since those are proof-oriented). Ensure the document encourages developing a **broad problem-solving mindset** rather than just isolated skill application.

## Final Assessment

The revised 40-technique document (with the recommended additions and fixes) will provide an **excellent foundation for AMC 12 and COMC preparation**, covering most recurring themes and methods up to 2024. It corrects the earlier overestimation of coverage --- realistically, even 50 techniques cannot guarantee solving every problem, given the contests' vast range of 300+ concepts[\[2\]](https://data.mendeley.com/datasets/b57t9psp65#:~:text=This%20is%20a%20primary%20dataset,Probability). However, focusing on these high-yield techniques will prepare students for a large majority of questions and give them tools to creatively tackle the unfamiliar ones.

It's important to stress that **breadth and adaptability** matter. Students should use the document as a launchpad: each technique is a door to a wider area of exploration. Where possible, encourage learning the reasoning behind techniques, not just the formula or procedure. For example, understanding *why* inclusion-exclusion works or how *Vieta's formulas* are derived makes it easier to apply them in novel situations.

For COMC, the document plus updates is equally relevant, with the caveat that COMC's format requires writing full solutions. Thus, students should practice not just applying the techniques but also explaining them clearly. Having a strong grip on these techniques will allow students to justify steps in a proof (e.g., citing the Pigeonhole Principle in a COMC solution, or clearly delineating a constructive counting argument).

**Bottom line:** Your document is on track to be a comprehensive guide. With the misinformation corrected (e.g. actual topic weights and realistic technique frequencies[\[1\]](https://blog.thethinkacademy.com/mastering-the-amc-10-insights-from-2022-and-strategies-for-2023/#:~:text=2023%20blog,and%20number%20theory%20%2812)[\[3\]](https://www.indigoresearch.org/blog/amc-12-guide-questions-scores#:~:text=,%E2%80%93%20PIE%2C%20bijections%2C%20constructive%20recursion)) and the strategic additions made, it will reflect the true demands of the AMC 12 and COMC as of 2025. Students who master all the listed techniques (and understand how to combine them) should be well-equipped to score in the top tier of these competitions, while also laying a solid groundwork for the next levels (AIME and Canadian Mathematical Olympiad) should they advance. Keep the content updated annually, as contest trends can evolve, and continue to seek feedback from recent test-takers. With these improvements, the techniques document will remain a highly valuable resource in the contest preparation community.
