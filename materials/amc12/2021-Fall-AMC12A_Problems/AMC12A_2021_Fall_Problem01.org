Problem
What is the value of $\frac{(2112-2021)^2}{169}$ ?
$\textbf{(A) } 7 \qquad\textbf{(B) } 21 \qquad\textbf{(C) } 49 \qquad\textbf{(D) } 64 \qquad\textbf{(E) } 91$
Solution 1 (Laws of Exponents)
We have

\[\frac{(2112-2021)^2}{169}=\frac{91^2}{169}=\frac{91^2}{13^2}=\left(\frac{91}{13}\right)^2=7^2=\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 49}.\]

~MRENTHUSIASM
Solution 2 (Difference of Squares)
We have

\[\frac{(2112-2021)^2}{169}=\frac{91^2}{169}=\frac{(10^2-3^2)^2}{13^2}=\frac{((10+3)(10-3))^2}{13^2}=\frac{(13\cdot7)^2}{13^2}=\frac{13^2 \cdot 7^2}{13^2}=7^2=\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 49}.\]
Solution 3 (Estimate)
We know that $2112-2021 = 91$ . Approximate this as $100$ as it is pretty close to it. Also, approximate $169$ to $170$ . We then have

\[\frac{(2112 - 2021)^2}{169} \approx \frac{100^2}{170} \approx \frac{1000}{17} \approx 58.\]

Now check the answer choices. The two closest answers are $49$ and $64$ . As the numerator is actually bigger than it should be, it should be the smaller answer, or $\boxed{\textbf{(C) } 49}$ .
