\section*{Problem Statement}

\begin{keyinsightbox}
The key insight is recognizing that to minimize $S = \frac{1}{r(1-r)}$, we need to maximize the denominator $r(1-r)$. \section*{Complete Solution}

\textbf{Step 1}: Express sum in terms of common ratio

A geometric series $a, ar, ar^2, \ldots$ has sum $S = \frac{a}{1-r}$ for $|r| < 1$.

Given: Second term $ar = 1$, so $a = \frac{1}{r}$.

Substituting: $S = \frac{1/r}{1-r} = \frac{1}{r(1-r)}$

\textbf{Step 2}: Apply AM-GM inequality

For positive numbers $r$ and $1-r$:
\[\frac{r + (1-r)}{2} \geq \sqrt{r(1-r)}\]

This gives: $\frac{1}{2} \geq \sqrt{r(1-r)}$

Squaring: $\frac{1}{4} \geq r(1-r)$

\textbf{Step 3}: Find minimum of $S$

Since $r(1-r) \leq \frac{1}{4}$, we have:
\[S = \frac{1}{r(1-r)} \geq \frac{1}{1/4} = 4\]

Equality occurs when $r = 1-r$, which gives $r = \frac{1}{2}$.

\textbf{Step 4}: Verify the minimum

When $r = \frac{1}{2}$: $a = \frac{1}{r} = 2$

Check: $ar = 2 \cdot \frac{1}{2} = 1$ $\checkmark$

Sum: $S = \frac{2}{1-\frac{1}{2}} = \frac{2}{\frac{1}{2}} = 4$ $\checkmark$

Therefore, the minimum value of $S$ is $\boxed{4}$.
