Problem
Let $n$ be the smallest positive integer such that $n$ is divisible by $20$ , $n^2$ is a perfect cube, and $n^3$ is a perfect square. What is the number of digits of $n$ ?
$\textbf{(A)}\ 3 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 4 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 5 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\ 6 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 7$
Solution
We know that $n^2 = k^3$ and $n^3 = m^2$ . Cubing and squaring the equalities respectively gives $n^6 = k^9 = m^4$ . Let $a = n^6$ . Now we know $a$ must be a perfect $36$ -th power because $lcm(9,4) = 36$ , which means that $n$ must be a perfect $6$ -th power. The smallest number whose sixth power is a multiple of $20$ is $10$ , because the only prime factors of $20$ are $2$ and $5$ , and $10 = 2 \times 5$ . Therefore our is equal to number $10^6 = 1000000$ , with $7$ digits $\Rightarrow \boxed {E}$ .
Solution 2 (Chinese Remainder Theorem)
Let $n = 2^2 \cdot 5 \cdot x$ for some integer $x$ , then we know that Since we have $2^4 \cdot 5^2 \cdot x^2$ a power of 3, that means, by the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic, the exponents of the primes must each be divisible by 3. This also means that there's no point in $p \mid x$ if $p \not \in \{2,3\}$ , since that would just serve to increase $n$ and we want $n$ to be minimal.
This means we can write $x = 2^{\alpha} \cdot 5^{\beta}$ and thus we have $2^4 \cdot 5^2 \cdot 2^{2\alpha} \cdot 5^{2\beta} = 2^{4 + 2\alpha} \cdot 5^{2 + 2\beta} = m_0^3$ , therefore we know and doing the same with the third equation being a square, we have We can shift this around into two systems involving the same variables, as follows: and And using Chinese Remainder Theorem to solve this, we get $\alpha = 4$ and $\beta = 5$ , and plugging that back in yields $n = 2^6 \cdot 5^6$ .
