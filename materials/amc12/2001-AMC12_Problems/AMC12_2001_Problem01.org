Problem
The sum of two numbers is $S$ . Suppose $3$ is added to each number and then
each of the resulting numbers is doubled. What is the sum of the final two
numbers?
$\textbf{(A)}\ 2S + 3\qquad \textbf{(B)}\ 3S + 2\qquad \textbf{(C)}\ 3S + 6 \qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 2S + 6 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\ 2S + 12$
Solution
Suppose the two numbers are $a$ and $b$ , with $a+b=S$ .
Then the desired sum is $2(a+3)+2(b+3)=2(a+b)+12=2S +12$ , which is answer $\boxed{\textbf{(E)}}$ .
