Problem
The state income tax where Kristin lives is charged at the rate of $p\%$ of the first $\textdollar 28000$ of annual income plus $(p + 2)\%$ of any amount above $\textdollar 28000$ . Kristin
noticed that the state income tax she paid amounted to $(p + 0.25)\%$ of her
annual income. What was her annual income?
$\textbf{(A)}\,\textdollar 28000 \qquad \textbf{(B)}\,\textdollar 32000 \qquad \textbf{(C)}\,\textdollar 35000 \qquad \textbf{(D)}\,\textdollar 42000 \qquad \textbf{(E)}\,\textdollar 56000$
Solution 1
Let $A$ , $T$ be Kristin's annual income and the income tax total, respectively. Notice that 

\begin{align*} T &= p\%\cdot28000 + (p + 2)\%\cdot(A - 28000) \\ &= [p\%\cdot28000 + p\%\cdot(A - 28000)] + 2\%\cdot(A - 28000) \\ &= p\%\cdot A + 2\%\cdot(A - 28000) \end{align*}

 We are also given that

\[T = (p + 0.25)\%\cdot A = p\%\cdot A + 0.25\%\cdot A\]

Thus,

\[p\%\cdot A + 2\%\cdot(A - 28000) = p\%\cdot A + 0.25\%\cdot A\]

\[2\%\cdot(A - 28000) = 0.25\%\cdot A\]

Solve for $A$ to obtain $A = \boxed{\textbf{(B) }\textdollar 32000}$ .
~ Nafer
Note: You may also assume p = 0 for easy calculation.
