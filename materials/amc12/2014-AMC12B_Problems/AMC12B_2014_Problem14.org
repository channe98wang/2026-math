Problem 14
A rectangular box has a total surface area of 94 square inches. The sum of the lengths of all its edges is 48 inches. What is the sum of the lengths in inches of all of its interior diagonals?
$\textbf{(A)}\ 8\sqrt{3}\qquad\textbf{(B)}\ 10\sqrt{2}\qquad\textbf{(C)}\ 16\sqrt{3}\qquad\textbf{(D)}\ 20\sqrt{2}\qquad\textbf{(E)}\ 40\sqrt{2}$
Solution 1
Let the side lengths of the rectangular box be $x, y$ and $z$ . 
From the information we get
\[4(x+y+z) = 48 \Rightarrow x+y+z = 12\]
\[2(xy+yz+xz) = 94\]
The sum of all the lengths of the box's interior diagonals is
\[4 \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}\]
Squaring the first expression, we get:
Hence

\[x^2+y^2+z^2 = 50\]
\[4 \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2} = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 20\sqrt 2}\]
Solution 2 (Observation)
Observe that a rectangular prism with side lengths of $3,4,5$ fits all the necessary conditions. Thus, the answer is

\[4\sqrt{3^2+4^2+5^2} = \boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 20\sqrt 2}\]

~LuisFonseca123
Solution 3 (Estimation?)
Observe that a cube with side length $4$ has an equal sum of edge lengths and a similar surface area $(96)$ . We can evaluate the total interior diagonal length of this cube to be $16\sqrt{3}$ . Since the cube almost follows the problem's conditions, we can infer that the total interior diagonal length of the rectangular prism to be close but unequal to $16\sqrt{3}$ , which leads us to an answer of

\[\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 20\sqrt 2}\]
Solution 4 (Guessinggggggg)
Observe that it just cannot be E, because this is way out of the others range. Next, observe that it cannot be B because it is not only pretty far away from C, but it is also the only one that is not divisible by 4. So we have 10, 16, 20 left. Note that the average of all the answers is highly skewed to the right of the median, therefore the answer will be around there (this is because the average of the answers is a good answer for MAA to use because it means that it is not obviously lower or higher than all 4 of the other answers, plus it is easier to get mislead with B or D.) With that being said, we can conclude that the answer is almost certainly D, because the average will be somewhat close to

\[\boxed{\textbf{(D)}\ 20\sqrt 2}\]
